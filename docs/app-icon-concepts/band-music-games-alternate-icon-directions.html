10 Alternate App Icon Directions

Non-record-player ideas built around the premise: band songs become games you play with other people.

Creative Direction

These directions drop the turntable constraint and explore more direct hybrids of music, games, party play, and the current song-color palette. They are intentionally broader so the next brand decision is not trapped inside vinyl metaphors.

Prototype First

Prototype Cassette Controller, Arcade Stage, and Drum Pad Grid first. They are the clearest at icon size and give three distinct brand positions: retro-playful, premise-literal, and modern music-tech.

01

Cassette Controller

A cassette shell doubles as a game controller, with tape reels as thumbsticks and song colors as buttons.

Build note: Strongest direct blend of music plus play; instantly reads as retro without using the record player.

Best for: A broad, approachable icon for the app premise.

02

Arcade Stage

An arcade cabinet becomes a tiny stage, with equalizer bars and spotlight beams inside the screen.

Build note: Communicates band songs becoming playable mini-games.

Best for: Most explicit 'songs to play games to' idea.

03

Drum Pad Grid

A compact drum-pad controller turns into a four-button game board using the app song colors.

Build note: Simple geometry, strong small-size read, and very easy to implement natively.

Best for: Modern, music-tech, highly scalable icon direction.

04

Guitar Pick Maze

A guitar pick contains a tiny maze path and a glowing play target.

Build note: It avoids generic music-note branding while staying close to band culture.

Best for: A cleaner, less arcade-specific brand mark.

05

Song Dice

Two rounded dice use music/game symbols as pips, suggesting random party challenges from songs.

Build note: Clear party-game energy, but risks feeling tabletop if not paired with song colors.

Best for: Emphasizing group play and replayability.

06

Playlist Trophy

A trophy cup is built from stacked playlist bars, making winning feel tied to music selection.

Build note: Good for the competitive game layer; less obvious as music at very small sizes.

Best for: A game-achievement direction.

07

Jukebox Token

A token/coin bears a simplified jukebox arch and colored track lanes.

Build note: Premium, simple, and app-icon-friendly while remaining music-first.

Best for: A more mature icon that still has party-game warmth.

08

Pinball Note

A music note becomes a pinball lane with bumpers and a bright ball.

Build note: High-energy and game-specific without relying on controllers.

Best for: A kinetic icon that can become animation in the app.

09

Band Board

A square board-game tile contains four instrument/player lanes converging on a center play button.

Build note: Represents multiple bands/songs/games in one modular mark.

Best for: A system icon that can scale as the catalog grows.

10

Headphone Crown

Headphones form a crown over three game gems, making the player the party DJ.

Build note: More abstract and brandable, but less literal about gameplay.

Best for: A consumer-app direction with stronger personality.
